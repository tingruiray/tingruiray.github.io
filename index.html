About

I'm a master's student in Computational Social Science (Economics) at the University of Chicago and a research assistant at the Center for the Economics of Human Development. Before Chicago I read Applied Economics at the Chinese University of Hong Kong, Shenzhen, and spent a year as a visiting student in Economics at Pembroke College, University of Oxford.

My research sits where development, labor, and behavioral economics meet. I'm drawn to questions about how early-life conditions and family structure leave long shadows on adult outcomes — wages, fertility, human capital — and I like building these questions out into models that can be taken to microdata and identified credibly. Recent work spans China's gender wage gap and the long-run fertility consequences of childhood parental separation among "left-behind" children.

Research and projects

Research proposal 2026 University of Chicago
Division of the Social Sciences

Migration, Childhood Parental Separation, and Adult Fertility in China: Evidence from CHARLS

Three-generation model CHARLS IV · Bartik Within-family FE

Builds a three-generation household model linking China's "left-behind children" phenomenon to the country's contemporary fertility decline. Childhood parental separation is allowed to shape adult fertility through two testable channels — human-capital formation and family attachment — and the empirical strategy pairs an ID-card-rollout instrument and a Bartik destination-access design with a within-family fixed-effects estimator that exploits sibling differences in exposure.

Labor Economics

A GLM Analysis and Decomposition of China's Gender Wage Differential: Impact of COVID-19

GLM Oaxaca decomposition CNSDA 2017 / 2021

Estimates and decomposes China's gender wage gap before and after COVID-19 using national survey data. A generalized linear model puts the gap at roughly 38 log points in 2017 and 35 in 2021 — narrowing overall, yet the unexplained, discrimination-consistent share rises from about 73% to 81%, suggesting persistent and possibly deepening discrimination against female workers.
